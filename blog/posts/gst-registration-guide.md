# GST Registration: Everything You Need to Know

GST registration is the first step for any business that crosses the threshold turnover limit. Here's a clear breakdown of the entire process.

## Who Needs GST Registration?

You **must** register for GST if:

- Your **annual turnover** exceeds ₹40 lakhs (₹20 lakhs for service providers)
- You make **inter-state supplies** (regardless of turnover)
- You sell through **e-commerce platforms**
- You're a **casual taxable person** or **non-resident taxable person**
- You're required to pay tax under **reverse charge mechanism**

## Documents Required

### For Proprietorship
- PAN card of the proprietor
- Aadhaar card
- Photograph
- Bank account details (cancelled cheque / passbook)
- Address proof of business (electricity bill, rent agreement)

### For Company / LLP
- All of the above, plus:
- Certificate of Incorporation
- MOA / AOA / LLP Agreement
- Board Resolution authorizing signatory
- Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)

## Step-by-Step Registration Process

### Step 1: Visit the GST Portal

Go to [gst.gov.in](https://www.gst.gov.in/) and click on **"Register Now"** under the Taxpayers section.

### Step 2: Fill Part A

Enter your:
- **PAN number**
- **Email address**
- **Mobile number**

You'll receive OTPs on both. Verify them to get a **Temporary Reference Number (TRN)**.

### Step 3: Fill Part B

Log in with your TRN and complete the detailed application:

1. **Business Details** — Legal name, trade name, constitution
2. **Promoter/Partner Details** — Personal info of all promoters
3. **Authorized Signatory** — Who will sign on behalf
4. **Principal Place of Business** — Address with proof
5. **Bank Account Details** — At least one active account
6. **Goods & Services** — HSN/SAC codes of what you deal in

### Step 4: Verification

- Sign using **DSC** (for companies/LLPs)
- Or verify via **Aadhaar OTP** / **EVC** (for proprietors)

### Step 5: Get Your GSTIN

Processing typically takes **3-7 working days**. Once approved, you'll receive your 15-digit GSTIN.

## Types of GST Registration

| Type | Best For |
|------|----------|
| **Regular** | Most businesses above threshold |
| **Composition Scheme** | Small businesses (turnover up to ₹1.5 Cr) wanting simplified compliance |
| **Voluntary** | Businesses below threshold wanting input tax credit |

## After Registration — What's Next?

Once registered, you must:

- File **GSTR-1** (outward supplies) by the **11th of every month**
- File **GSTR-3B** (summary return) by the **20th of every month**
- Maintain proper **invoicing** with GST details
- Keep records for **6 years**
