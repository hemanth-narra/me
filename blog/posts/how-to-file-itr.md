# How to File Your ITR Online — A Simple Guide

Filing your Income Tax Return doesn't have to be stressful. Here's a no-nonsense walkthrough of the entire process.

## Who Needs to File?

If any of the following apply to you, you're required to file an ITR:

- Your **gross total income** exceeds ₹2.5 lakhs (₹3 lakhs for senior citizens)
- You want to **claim a refund** from the Income Tax Department
- You have **foreign assets or income**
- You want to apply for a **loan or visa** (banks and embassies often ask for ITR receipts)

## Which ITR Form Should You Use?

| Form | Who It's For |
|------|-------------|
| **ITR-1 (Sahaj)** | Salaried individuals with income up to ₹50 lakhs |
| **ITR-2** | Individuals with capital gains or foreign income |
| **ITR-3** | Individuals with business/profession income |
| **ITR-4 (Sugam)** | Presumptive income scheme (small businesses) |

## Step-by-Step Process

### 1. Gather Your Documents

Before you start, keep these handy:

- **PAN Card**
- **Aadhaar Card** (linked to PAN)
- **Form 16** (from your employer)
- **Bank statements** (for interest income)
- **Investment proofs** (80C, 80D, etc.)

### 2. Log In to the IT Portal

Head to [incometax.gov.in](https://www.incometax.gov.in/) and log in with your PAN number.

### 3. Select the Right Form

Based on the table above, pick the appropriate ITR form. For most salaried individuals, **ITR-1** is the one.

### 4. Fill in Your Details

The portal pre-fills a lot of your data from Form 26AS and AIS. Verify everything:

- Personal information
- Salary details
- House property income (if any)
- Other sources (FD interest, savings interest)
- Deductions under Chapter VI-A

### 5. Verify and Submit

Once you've reviewed everything:

1. Calculate your tax liability
2. Pay any pending tax via challan
3. Submit the return
4. **E-verify** using Aadhaar OTP (easiest method)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- ❌ Not reporting **all bank accounts**
- ❌ Forgetting to declare **FD interest** income
- ❌ Not matching data with **Form 26AS / AIS**
- ❌ Missing the deadline (usually **July 31st**)
